# Week 4: Design Vocabulary: What Makes Games Games

> **How to use this module:** Everything you need to complete this week's work is in this module. The live lectures walk the same ground off script, so treat them as a bonus, not a requirement. Read the concept material, follow the practice guide in your engine, and check the assignment spec before you build.

**Role in the course:** A step back from code. Ask ten people what makes a game fun and you'll get ten shrugs. Designers have actual vocabulary for this, and you will use it all semester, directly in A12 (Deconstruction) and in your final project design doc.

## Learning objectives
- Define and identify a game's core loop.
- Use the Mechanics-Dynamics-Aesthetics (MDA) framework to analyze a game.
- Distinguish mechanics from systems from content.
- Analyze games across genres (platformer, roguelike, RTS, sim, puzzle) with the same vocabulary.

## Concept material (to be written)
1. **The Core Loop:** the 5 to 30 seconds a player repeats, and why everything else hangs off it.
2. **MDA:** mechanics (rules), dynamics (play), aesthetics (feelings). Designers write mechanics; players experience aesthetics. The gap between those two is where design lives.
3. Verbs, resources, and feedback loops (positive and negative).
4. **Genre Tour:** the same vocabulary applied to five very different games.
5. Scope: small, finished, and polished beats big and broken. Every time.

## Practice guide (to be written)

- A worked example deconstruction to model what A12 should look like.
