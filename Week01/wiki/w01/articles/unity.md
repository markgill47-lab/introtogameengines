# Unity

> **Language:** C# · **License:** free Personal tier · **Status in this course:** the default, and the one I teach in

*Opinions follow. They're mine, they're stated plainly, and reasonable professionals disagree with every one of them.*

The course default and the one I teach in. C# scripting, a mature editor, and the largest tutorial-and-answers ecosystem in existence, which matters more than students think: at 11 PM, your error message has already been solved on some forum, in Unity's case usually eleven times.

The Personal license is free below a revenue threshold you do not need to worry about yet.

**Its weakness is churn:** Unity has a habit of shipping two or three ways to do the same thing — two render pipelines, two [[input-system|input systems]], two [[ui-system|UI systems]] — and leaving both alive. I'll tell you which one we're using whenever it comes up.

Under the [[engine-policy|engine policy]], Unity students get their exact problem solved on screen, because the [[demo-game-w01|demo game]] is a Unity project. When in doubt in [[how-to-choose|How to Choose]]: Unity. It's the most-supported path in this course and the VizLab machines have it installed.
