# Unreal

> **Language:** C++ and Blueprints (visual scripting) · **License:** free for this course's purposes · **Status in this course:** choose it with your eyes open

The AAA engine, and it looks like it: the prettiest defaults in the business. C++ for real work, Blueprints for a great deal of legitimate work, and **Enhanced Input** that ships half of Week 12's [[input-system|input material]] in the box.

**The costs are real:** the largest install (50–100+ GB), the heaviest hardware demands, the slowest iteration loop, and C++ is the least forgiving language on this wiki.

Choose Unreal if you already have a machine that can carry it and a reason to want it. "It looks cool" is a reason; just budget the hours. Hardware is question one of [[how-to-choose|How to Choose]] for a reason — Unreal on a five-year-old laptop is a bad semester.
