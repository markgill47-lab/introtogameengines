# Godot

> **Language:** GDScript or C# · **License:** open source, free forever · **Status in this course:** a very good choice

Open source, free forever, no licensing asterisks, and refreshingly small: it downloads in seconds and runs on hardware that would make [[unreal|Unreal]] laugh.

GDScript is a Python-flavored language you'll pick up in an afternoon, and C# is supported if you'd rather stay close to the [[unity|Unity]] demos. Godot's **signals** are the Observer pattern as a first-class engine feature, which will make Week 11 feel like home turf.

**The trade-off:** the ecosystem is smaller than Unity's; your 11 PM error has been solved maybe twice, not eleven times. And under the [[engine-policy|engine policy]] you'll be translating the Unity demos — a skill this course explicitly values, but a tax you should choose on purpose.

A note for the [[llm-tutor|LLM-as-tutor]] workflow: Godot is the poster child for version drift. The API renamed heavily between Godot 3 and Godot 4, and chatbots constantly hand out Godot 3 syntax. Always say "Godot 4" in your prompts.
