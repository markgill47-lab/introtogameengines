# Week 4: Design Vocabulary, What Makes Games Games

> **How to use this module:** No assignment is paced this week (it is a catch-up week for [[w02:a1-spec|Assignment 1]] and [[w03:a2-spec|Assignment 2]]), but nothing here is optional filler. This vocabulary is the rubric for [[a12-template|Assignment 12]] (Game Deconstruction), the language of your final project's design doc, and the way we will talk about every game we touch for the rest of the semester.

**Role in the course:** A step back from code. Ask ten people what makes a game fun and you will get ten shrugs. Designers have actual vocabulary for this, and you will use it all semester. It is a code-free week, and it is not a soft week: bad vocabulary produces bad design docs, and bad design docs produce unbuildable projects.

## Learning objectives

- Define and identify a game's [[core-loop|core loop]].
- Use the [[mda|Mechanics-Dynamics-Aesthetics]] framework to analyze a game.
- Distinguish mechanics from systems from content.
- Analyze games across genres (platformer, roguelike, RTS, sim, puzzle) with [[genre-tour|the same vocabulary]].
