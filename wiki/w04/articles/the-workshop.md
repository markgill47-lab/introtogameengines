# The Deconstruction Workshop

The hands-on half of Week 4, except this week the hands hold a notepad instead of a keyboard. Nothing here is submitted this week. All of it is a dress rehearsal for [[a12-template|Assignment 12]], which you present in Week 15 on a game you choose. Start thinking about which one.

## Live session

We play a small game together on the projector (short, free, nobody needs prior experience), with the room calling out observations while one volunteer drives. We fill a whiteboard with [[a12-template|the template]], argue about what the core loop actually is (there will be argument; the argument is the point), and leave with a completed group deconstruction.

## Remote / async

Same exercise, self-serve. Play the same game (posted on D2L with a link the week of), fill in the template yourself, and post your version to the discussion board. Then read two other people's and reply where you disagree, because the live room gets the arguing for free and you deserve the same. Disagreeing about what a game's core loop is turns out to be one of the fastest ways to learn what a [[core-loop|core loop]] is.

## Suggested lab games

Short, cheap or free, dense with design: Tetris (any version), Vampire Survivors, Mini Metro, Into the Breach's opening hours, Celeste's first chapters, A Short Hike. The demo game's Week 4 session also includes the valley flythrough: the RPG's core loop stated out loud before a single mechanic exists, which is the order your final project should do it in too.
