# The Assignment 12 Template

Copy this skeleton for [[the-workshop|the workshop]], for practice, and eventually for Assignment 12 itself.

1. **Game / genre / platform / hours played.** (Assignment 12 expects enough play to be past first impressions. Two hours minimum, more for bigger games.)
2. **[[core-loop|Core loop]]**, one sentence, with its real-world duration in seconds. Nested loops if they matter, labeled by scale.
3. **[[verbs-resources-feedback|Verbs]]**, counted, with a sentence on few-deep vs. many-loose.
4. **Resources**, including at least one non-obvious one (time, space, information).
5. **Feedback loops**, at least one identified, labeled positive or negative, with the aesthetic it produces.
6. **One [[mda|MDA trace]]** from a feeling you actually had, back through the behavior, to the rule that caused it.
7. **Likely architecture**, one paragraph. (Week 15 version: name the patterns hiding in it. That is the "where the patterns are hiding" requirement in the assignment.)
8. **One design lesson you are stealing** for your own final project.

## Delivery

Paper (2 to 4 pages) or video (5 to 8 minutes), presented live in Week 15's studio or submitted as a recording. Same weight either way, as always.

*The [[tetris-deconstruction|worked Tetris example]] fills in every one of these fields, so read it as the benchmark for what "enough" looks like.*
