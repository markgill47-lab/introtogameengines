# Start with a Joke

A comedian writes a joke. What they actually write is *words*: a setup, a turn, a punchline, in a specific order. What they are trying to produce is a *laugh*. And here is the thing every comedian knows and every new game designer has to learn: **you cannot write a laugh.** There is no notation for it. You can only write words that, delivered in the right order at the right speed to the right room, reliably *cause* one. When the room does not laugh, you do not yell at the room. You rewrite the words.

Game design is this exact predicament, formalized. You cannot build "fun." There is no fun slider in Unity; I have looked. You can only build *rules* (this button jumps, enemies take three hits, gold buys arrows) that, when a player pushes against them, reliably produce an experience: tension, mastery, curiosity, triumph, panic. When players are bored, you do not yell at the players. You rewrite the rules.

This week is the vocabulary for talking about that gap between what you can build and what you are trying to cause. It is a code-free week, and it is not a soft week: bad vocabulary produces bad design docs, bad design docs produce unbuildable projects, and I have read enough of both to make this week mandatory with a clear conscience.
