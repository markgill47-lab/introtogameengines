# Game Programming Patterns

> **Author:** Robert Nystrom · **Read it:** [gameprogrammingpatterns.com](https://gameprogrammingpatterns.com/) · **License:** prose CC BY-NC-ND (read and share, don't republish modified copies); code samples MIT

The closest thing this course has to a required text, and the book the B tier is standing on: his chapters are literally named **Command**, **Observer**, and **State**, plus **Game Loop**, **Update Method**, and **Component**. Read it in the browser; if you love it, the paid copies fund an author who gave the field a free classic.

It's written in exactly the register this course uses: motivating problem first, pattern second, opinionated throughout.

## Where it pairs with the course

- **Week 3:** Game Loop and Update Method, alongside the loop module.
- **Week 9:** Component, which is the composition argument behind the [[w02:standard-prefab|Standard Prefab]], finally named.
- **Week 10:** State.
- **Week 11:** Observer.
- **Week 12:** Command. He goes deeper on undo and flexibility; the course goes deeper on engine wiring and the demo. Complementary, not redundant.

**One caveat:** it is a patterns book, not an engine book. No physics, UI, cameras, animation, or asset pipeline. That's what the rest of [[home|the library]] is for.

This is the book your B-tier students keep reading after the semester ends. Consider that a warning.
