# Gotchas and Judgment Calls

- **The silent trigger.** A zone with no rigid body anywhere in the pair, firing nothing, in complete silence. [[collision-vs-trigger|At least one body in the pair, forever]], and [[silent-gate|Exercise 3]] has you walk both directions through this trap before you record.
- **The shepherded dynamic body.** Moving a `Rigidbody` around with `transform.Translate` or position assignment. It sometimes looks fine in a video and it is still [[body-types|the cardinal rule]] broken. The source is the audit trail, and I do read it. If you need direct placement, change the citizenship instead of cheating the one you chose.
- **The dt-on-an-impulse bug, physics edition.** Multiplying a one-shot impulse by delta time, so your throw strength depends on the framerate of the frame you threw in. [[w03:gotchas|Week 3's version of this bug]] was about jumps; it is the same bug with a `Rigidbody` attached. Continuous: dt. Instantaneous: no dt.
- **Continuous force in the wrong clock.** Applying a per-step push from `Update` instead of `FixedUpdate`, so the strength of your thrust rides the framerate. [[forces-and-impulses|The handshake pattern]] exists precisely to keep each thing on its own clock.
- **The mislabeled kinematic.** An object called kinematic that never moves on camera (that is static) or that visibly gets shoved by something (that is dynamic). [[a3-spec|Requirement 1]] wants the citizenship *demonstrated*, not labeled. Show it pushing something and refusing to be pushed.
- **The trigger that is actually a wall.** `isTrigger` left unchecked, so the zone physically blocks the player while also firing events. That is a wall with opinions, not a tripwire, and it fails the half of [[a3-spec|Requirement 4]] that wants free passage.
- **Colliders sized by scaling the prefab parent.** This shears the physics world away from the visual one and produces bugs that look like the engine lying to you. [[choosing-colliders|Size the collider]]. [[w02:standard-prefab|Week 2's (1, 1, 1) rule]] was also a physics rule; now you know.
- **The ball that thuds.** A newly handed-over body arrives with zero velocity and zero spin, so an object you expected to sail away instead drops where it stood. The simulation cannot infer speed from the positions your script was writing last frame, because [[bowling-alley|a position is not a velocity]]. If it should be moving at the handoff, you have to supply that yourself.
- **The rigid body with no collider.** An object that has agreed to obey gravity and has nothing to land on. It falls forever, silently, and the Inspector looks completely reasonable.
