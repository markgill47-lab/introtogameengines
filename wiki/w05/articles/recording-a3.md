# Recording Your Assignment 3 Video

A shape that fits 2–5 minutes:

1. **Citizenships (45 sec):** the crate tower settling and toppling. Point at the ground (static), a crate (dynamic), and demonstrate the kinematic by having it push something and refuse to be pushed back. *(Covers [[a3-spec|Requirement 1]].)*
2. **The throw (60 sec):** code walkthrough of [[the-throw|the thrower]] hitting the four decisions (edge input, impulse, no dt, why it is allowed in `Update`), then rocks doing damage on camera. *(Requirement 2, and Requirement 3 arrives for free when the tower goes down.)*
3. **Tripwire vs. wall (60 sec):** walk [[silent-gate|the gate]] (through it, event fires), then rock the crates (off them, physics fires). If you are brave, show the silent version first and add the Rigidbody live; the fix landing on camera is excellent evidence you understand it. *(Requirement 4.)*
4. **The matrix (30 sec):** [[the-matrix|point-blank throw]], rocks ignoring you, one sentence about the checkbox. *(Requirement 5.)*
5. **Wrap (15 sec):** what fought you, what fixed it.

Then the build note: what AI produced, what you fixed, what you would change. One paragraph. Done.

## The narration note for this week specifically

Requirements 2 and 4 are the two requirements students lose on while showing a scene that works fine.

Both are graded on the *explanation*, not just the effect. A rock that flies across the screen does not prove you know why it was an impulse, and a gate that turns green does not prove you know why it is not a wall. Say the reasoning out loud. It costs you eight seconds each and it is the difference between a pass and a resubmit.
