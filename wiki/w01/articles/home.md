# Week 1: Engines, Anatomy, and Choosing Yours

> **How to use this module:** Everything you need to complete this week's work is in these articles. The live lectures walk the same ground off script, so treat them as a bonus, not a requirement. Read the concept articles, follow the [[install-checklists|practice guide]] in your engine, and check [[this-weeks-work|this week's work]] before you build.

**Role in the course:** Orientation. Before you write a line of code, you should know what a game engine actually is, what every engine has in common, and which one you'll live in all semester.

## Learning objectives

- Describe the major subsystems every engine provides: the [[common-anatomy|Common Anatomy]].
- Compare [[unity|Unity]], [[godot|Godot]], [[unreal|Unreal]], and [[threejs|Three.js]]: languages, licensing, platform targets, strengths.
- Install and verify a working development environment in your chosen engine: the [[hello-scene|Hello Scene]].
- Use an LLM as a tutor for [[llm-tutor|learning an unfamiliar engine]].

## Where to start

1. [[start-with-2002|Start with 2002]]: why engines exist, and the most important idea in this module.

5. [[this-weeks-work|This Week's Work]]: no paced assignment; the deliverable is a working development environment and a decision.
