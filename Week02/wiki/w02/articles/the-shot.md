# Exercise 4: The Shot (10 minutes)

A [[camera-as-object|camera]] placed like you meant it.

1. Place the camera to frame your village and windmill deliberately: slightly elevated, looking down the main street, windmill breaking the skyline. Use the scene view to fly somewhere that looks good, then align the camera to your view (GameObject → Align With View, and learn that hotkey).
2. Adjust field of view once, just to see what it does: 40 is a calm lens, 90 is a wide anxious one.
3. For your narration, have one sentence ready about *why* the camera is where it is. "I wanted the windmill visible over the roofline" is a design decision, and it's precisely the sentence [[a1-spec|A1's R5]] asks for.

*That's all four exercises. A1 doesn't require a house or a windmill specifically; it requires the concepts these exercises install. Build these, then build your own scene, or build these **as** your scene. Both are fine. The spec is the spec: [[a1-spec|A1: Scenes & Transforms]].*
